Paper Title: Multimodal Framework for Deep Fake Detection and Content Moderation Using CNN, ViT, and Audio-Visual Analysis 
Authors: Anoushka Panja, Debamalya Chakravorty, Diganta Saha, and Arijit Das 
Year: 2025 

1. Problem Addressed:
The rise of sophisticated deepfake technologies enables the creation of hyper-realistic synthetic media that deceives traditional detection. Existing systems often rely on single-modal approaches that are less robust against diverse forms of malicious content, including hate speech in regional languages.
2. Methodology:
The paper proposes a modular pipeline for real-time multimedia analysis:Visual Analysis: Uses CNNs (ResNet, VGG16) for spatial artifacts and Vision Transformers (ViT) to capture global and long-range dependencies across video frames.Audio Analysis: Extracts MFCCs and spectrograms; uses RNNs/CNNs to identify synthetic voice patterns and temporal anomalies.Text/Content Moderation: Employs OCR (Tesseract) to extract text from frames and BERT for hate speech classification.Regional Support: Integrates Bengali speech-to-text to handle low-resource regional languages.Fusion: Implements a late fusion strategy to combine audio and video probabilistic scores into a final classification.
3. Key Findings:
The framework achieved 95.4% accuracy in video deepfake detection and 93.5% in audio deepfake detection.Hate speech detection reached an F1-score of 90.7%.The integration of ViT was specifically noted for its effectiveness in capturing complex visual anomalies that traditional CNNs might miss.
4. Limitations:
Fusion Techniques: The authors suggest that while late fusion is effective, more advanced attention-based fusion could further improve performance.Adversarial Robustness: The system needs more development to remain reliable in adversarial environments where creators intentionally try to bypass detection.Language Scope: While it includes Bengali, the framework needs expansion to other regional/low-resource languages to be truly inclusive.
