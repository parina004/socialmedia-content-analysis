Paper Title: Detection of GAN-Generated Images by Estimating Artifact Similarity 
Authors: Weichuang Li, Peisong He, Haoliang Li, Hongxia Wang, and Ruimei Zhang 
Year: 2022 

1. Problem Addressed: 
The paper tackles the restricted performance of AI-detection models when facing unseen GAN models (cross-domain scenarios) and their lack of robustness against common post-processing like JPEG compression or resizing.
2. Methodology: 
The authors propose GASE-Net, which uses a two-stage process: representation learning and representation comparison. It uses a ResNet-50 backbone with Instance Normalization in shallow layers to extract generalized features and a "Category and Domain-Aware" (CDA) loss function to better distinguish between real and AI-generated artifacts.
3. Key Findings: 
GASE-Net outperformed state-of-the-art methods in cross-domain tests, particularly on Transformer-based GANs, where it surpassed others by over 30%. It also showed high robustness to Gaussian blur and resizing even without data augmentation during training.
4. Limitations: 
Detection accuracy still decreases as post-processing intensity increases (e.g., very low-quality JPEG compression). The method requires a "reference set" of known GAN images to calculate prototypes for comparison.
