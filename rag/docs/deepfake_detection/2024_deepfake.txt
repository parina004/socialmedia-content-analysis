Paper Title: Domain-Adaptive Representation Learning for Multimodal Deepfake Detection 
Authors: Ijaz Ul Haq and Khalid M. Malik 
Year: 2025 

1. Problem Addressed:
The authors target the difficulty of detecting multimodal deepfakes (audio-visual) that traditional unimodal detectors miss. They specifically address the "generalization gap," where models fail to detect unseen forgeries or adapt across different social media domains.
2. Methodology:
The authors propose Domain-Adaptive Representation Learning (DARL).Feature Extraction: Uses pretrained speech recognition models (wav2vec2.0 for audio and AV-HuBERT for visual/lips) to extract domain-specific embeddings.Representation Learning: Employs a Deep Canonical Correlation Analysis (DCCA) network to find the maximum correlation between lip movements and spoken words.Detection: Analyzes the "misalignment" between audio and video as a forensic signature.
3. Key Findings:
Facial manipulation inevitably disturbs the natural correlation between lip movement and audio.The DARL-L2 (two-layer) configuration provides the optimal balance for detecting deepfakes with high accuracy (reaching 99.95% on some datasets).The model is language-independent because it analyzes embedding-level correlations rather than specific phonemes.
4. Limitations:
The model struggles slightly with low-quality (LQ) visual modalities, as seen in its runner-up performance on the DF-TIMIT (LQ) dataset. It also shows minor confusion between certain subclasses in multiclass scenarios.
